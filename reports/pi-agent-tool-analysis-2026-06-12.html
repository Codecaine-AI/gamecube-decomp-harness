Recommendation: use a 90-minute nominal cutoff for the next pruning sweep. A lease still alive at 90 min has a 21% chance of ever confirming — a fresh lease off the queue runs at 51%. Killing at 90 min keeps 366/380 (96%) of confirmed successes and frees ~28 worker-hours per ~700 leases. 75 min is the yield-optimized setting (keeps 94%, frees 48 h), but it puts more legitimate work at risk. A strict 90-minute hard kill would currently interrupt 1 confirmed exact at the edge of the sample, so use a drain grace or set --agent-timeout-seconds 5700 if the implementation kills exactly on the second.

The knob already exists: --agent-timeout-seconds bounds each live Pi session and currently defaults to no timeout (apps/cli/src/cli/usage.ts:42). For a nominal 90-minute limit, set --agent-timeout-seconds 5400. *Net column assumes freed hours are re-spent on fresh leases at the 51% base rate and 36-min mean success duration.

Method & caveats. Outcomes from events (latest terminal worker/report event per lease: worker_*, needs_fact, or score_candidate; runner_validation canonical) joined to pi_sessions and the worker JSONL transcripts in .pi-sessions/worker/ for durations and tool calls; repair sessions are summed into their lease.
