Under the matches-only shipping policy, improvements stay local as branch delta — so their value is as stepping stones toward exact. Recommendation: kill at 75 minutes. A lease still alive at 75 min has a 22% chance of ever confirming — a fresh lease off the queue runs at 51%. Killing at 75 min keeps 358/379 (94%) of confirmed successes and frees ~48 worker-hours per ~600 leases. 60 min is the aggressive setting (keeps 90%, frees 78 h) — net expected yield is about the same; 75 min is safer against medium-size targets that legitimately need the time.

The knob already exists: --agent-timeout-seconds bounds each live Pi session and currently defaults to no timeout (apps/cli/src/cli/usage.ts:42). Set --agent-timeout-seconds 4500 on worker launch. *Net column assumes freed hours are re-spent on fresh leases at the 51% base rate and 36-min mean success duration.

Method & caveats. Outcomes from events (latest worker_* event per lease; runner_validation canonical) joined to pi_sessions and the worker JSONL transcripts in .pi-sessions/worker/ for durations and tool calls; repair sessions are summed into their lease. Missing legacy transcript paths from the old checkout are remapped by filename into the repo-local worker transcript directory. Medium-thinking leases (run 1 only) are excluded everywhere — the fleet is all-xhigh now. "Confirmed" = result accepted by runner validation (worker_finished + passed). Run 2 was still active when sampled (4 in-flight leases excluded). Tool lift is correlational, not causal; the kill-threshold table is the survival-style read (P(confirm | still running at T)) and is robust to that. Generated by analysis/scripts/analyze-pi-agent-tools.py + analysis/scripts/render-pi-agent-tool-report.py.
